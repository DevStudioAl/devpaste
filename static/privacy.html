Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026 · Effective immediately · Applies to all users of DevPaste

✅

Short version: We store only encrypted data we cannot read. We do not track you, profile you, sell your data, or use cookies. The encryption key never reaches our server. This policy exists to be transparent about the very little data we do handle.

1 Who We Are

DevPaste is a zero-knowledge encrypted secret-sharing service operated by DevStudioAl (devstudioal.com). When this policy says "we", "us", or "our", it refers to DevStudioAl.

If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at: privacy@devstudioal.com

2 The Zero-Knowledge Architecture

Understanding how DevPaste works is essential to understanding this privacy policy:

  • When you create a paste, your browser generates a random AES-256-GCM encryption key locally.
  • Your content is encrypted in your browser before it is sent to our server.
  • Only the ciphertext (encrypted, unreadable data) is transmitted to and stored on our server.
  • The decryption key is placed in the #fragment of the share URL. Browsers never send the #fragment to the server — this is defined by the HTTP specification.
  • We cannot decrypt, read, or access the content of any paste. Not now, not ever.

3 What Data We Store

The following table describes every field stored in our database for each paste:

Field What it is Readable by us?
id A random 32-character alphanumeric identifier Yes — but meaningless alone
encrypted_content AES-256-GCM ciphertext of your content No — unreadable without the key
iv Cryptographic initialization vector (not secret) Yes — but useless without the key
password_hash bcrypt hash of your optional password (if set) No — one-way hash, cannot be reversed
expires_at Unix timestamp when the paste will be deleted Yes
burn_after_read Flag: whether to delete on first view Yes
created_at Unix timestamp of paste creation Yes

We do not store: your IP address in the database, your name, email, browser, device, location, or any identifier linked to you personally.

4 IP Addresses and Rate Limiting

To prevent brute-force attacks on password-protected pastes, we apply rate limiting. This requires temporarily holding your IP address in server memory.

Important: IP addresses used for rate limiting are held in memory only — they are never written to the database, never logged to disk, and are automatically cleared after 10 minutes or upon a successful unlock. They are not used for any other purpose.

Server access logs (if any) generated by the hosting infrastructure may capture IP addresses at the network level. These are outside our direct control and are subject to the hosting provider's own retention and privacy policies.

5 Cookies and Tracking

  • We do not use cookies of any kind.
  • We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting.
  • We do not use advertising networks.
  • We do not track you across sessions or devices.
  • We have no way to identify returning users.

6 Third-Party Services

DevPaste loads fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). When your browser loads a font, Google may receive your IP address and browser information as part of the request. This is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

No other third-party services, scripts, CDNs, or external resources are loaded by DevPaste. There are no social media buttons, no embedded iframes from third parties, and no external JavaScript.

7 Data Retention and Deletion

All pastes are automatically and permanently deleted based on your chosen expiry setting:

  • 5 minutes — deleted 5 minutes after creation
  • 1 hour — deleted 1 hour after creation
  • 1 day — deleted 24 hours after creation
  • 7 days — deleted 7 days after creation
  • 30 days — deleted 30 days after creation (maximum)

If Burn After Read is enabled, the paste is deleted immediately after it is viewed for the first time — regardless of the expiry setting.

Deletion is permanent. There is no archive, no backup of paste content, and no way to recover a deleted paste.

8 Security Measures

The following technical security controls are in place:

  • AES-256-GCM encryption — military-grade authenticated encryption performed entirely in your browser
  • bcrypt password hashing — passwords are never stored in plaintext; only a one-way hash is kept
  • Content Security Policy — prevents scripts from sending data to external servers
  • Referrer-Policy: no-referrer — the decryption key in the URL fragment cannot leak via HTTP referrer headers
  • X-Frame-Options: DENY — prevents clickjacking attacks
  • Brute-force protection — password unlock is rate-limited to 10 attempts per 10 minutes per IP
  • Payload size limits — requests are capped to prevent denial-of-service attacks

9 Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right of access — the right to request what personal data we hold about you
  • Right to erasure — the right to request deletion of your data
  • Right to restriction — the right to request that we restrict processing
  • Right to object — the right to object to processing
  • Right to data portability — the right to receive your data in a machine-readable format

In practice, because DevPaste stores no personal identifiers, we have no technical way to link stored data to a specific individual. A paste ID alone is not sufficient to establish identity. We cannot connect an IP address (which we do not store) to a paste.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@devstudioal.com.

10 Children's Privacy

DevPaste is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect any data from children under 16. If you believe a child under 16 has used this service, please contact us and we will take appropriate action.
